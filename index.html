Introduction

The web browser is one of the most important applications in the modern computing landscape. It serves as a gateway to the vast universe of the internet, enabling users to view websites, run applications, and interact with content.

From the early days of Netscape Navigator to today’s feature-rich applications, browsers have evolved significantly in speed, standards compliance, and usability.

Learn more from this Wikipedia article.

A brief history of IE

Internet Explorer (IE) was Microsoft’s browser, launched in 1995. It dominated the market in the early 2000s due to its bundling with Windows.

However, it fell behind in speed and standards compliance. Microsoft eventually replaced it with Edge.

Learn more at Microsoft Edge’s website.

A brief history of Firefox

Mozilla Firefox was launched in 2004 as an open-source alternative. It focused on speed, security, and adherence to web standards.

Firefox helped break IE's monopoly and gained popularity for its extensions and developer tools.

Visit mozilla.org to learn more.

A brief history of Chrome

Google Chrome debuted in 2008 and quickly became the dominant browser. It was praised for its speed, simplicity, and V8 JavaScript engine.

Chrome now forms the basis of many other browsers through its open-source core, Chromium.

Visit Google Chrome’s homepage.
